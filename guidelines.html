What is Community Publishing?
Instant publishing for your creative apps

Community Publishing allows you to share your apps instantly with the community without waiting for approval. Your apps will appear in the "Community" tab of the App Store immediately after submission.

With great freedom comes great responsibility - please ensure your apps comply with all guidelines to maintain a positive environment for all users.

Key Requirements
Essential guidelines at a glance
  • Original Work: Only submit apps you created yourself
  • Safe Content: No malicious code, offensive content, or harmful material
  • Size Limit: Apps must be under 1MB when encoded
  • Privacy: Respect user data and privacy
  • Functionality: Apps must be functional and tested

shield Content Requirements

What you can and cannot include in your apps

1.1 Original Content

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Requirement: You must only submit apps that you have created yourself. Submitting stolen, copied, or plagiarized code will result in immediate removal of your app and potential ban from the platform.

  • All code, assets, and content must be created by you
  • If you use third-party libraries or frameworks, ensure they are properly licensed and credited
  • Include attribution for any open-source components used

1.2 Appropriate Content

Your app must not contain:

  • Malicious code, viruses, or any harmful software
  • Offensive, discriminatory, or hateful content
  • Sexually explicit or pornographic material
  • Content that promotes violence, illegal activities, or self-harm
  • Copyrighted material without proper authorization
  • Spam or misleading information

1.3 Privacy & Security

  • Do not collect user data without explicit consent
  • Do not include tracking scripts or analytics without disclosure
  • Protect any user input or stored data appropriately
  • Do not attempt to access or modify other users' data

code Technical Requirements

Size limits, metadata, and functionality standards

2.1 File Size Limits

‚ÑπÔ∏è Size Limit: Your app's HTML content must be less than 2500 characters or 1MB when encoded as Base64.

  • Optimize your code to stay within the size limit
  • Use external CDNs for large libraries when possible
  • Minify your HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

2.2 App Metadata

Your app must include:

  • Name: A clear, descriptive name (no offensive language)
  • Description: An accurate description of what your app does
  • Author: Your name or username
  • Version: A valid version number (e.g., 1.0.0)
  • Tags: Relevant tags to help users find your app
  • ID: A unique identifier (auto-generated but customizable for updates)

2.3 Functionality

  • Your app must be functional and not intentionally broken
  • Test your app before publishing
  • Ensure compatibility with modern web browsers
  • Handle errors gracefully

upload Publishing Process

How to publish and update your apps

3.1 From App Creator

‚úì Easy Publishing: Use the "Export for Publishing" button in the App Creator, then choose "Community" when prompted.

  1. Create your app in the App Creator
  2. Click "Export for Publishing"
  3. Choose "Publish to Community" (no approval needed)
  4. Enter your author name
  5. Confirm you've read and agree to these guidelines
  6. Enter the version number
  7. Add relevant tags for discoverability
  8. Acknowledge that the app is your original work
  9. Submit your app

3.2 Updates

  • To update an existing app, use the same App ID
  • Increment the version number (e.g., 1.0.0 ‚Üí 1.0.1)
  • Users will be notified of available updates

Understanding the difference between app types

Community Apps

  • Published instantly without approval

Featured Apps

  • Must meet higher quality standards
  • Displayed prominently in the App Store
  • Submit via the "Featured" option when exporting

gavel Moderation & Enforcement

How we keep the community safe

‚ö†Ô∏è Violations: Apps that violate these guidelines will be removed immediately. Repeated violations may result in a permanent ban from publishing.

5.1 Reporting

Users can report apps that violate these guidelines. All reports will be reviewed promptly.

5.2 Removal

Apps may be removed for:

  • Being non-functional or broken
